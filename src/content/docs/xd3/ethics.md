## Ethics

> Carefully concidering the **rights**, **wellfare** and **dignity** of people involved in your research.

> Applies to how you conduct your research and how you handle data from clients.

### Core features

- A research project must **not cause harm** (physical, emotional or moral) to anyone.
- In your approach, you do everything with **respect and honor** for the other person.

### Three moral values

#### Be honest

- Not trying to elicit answers from anyone.
- Don't try to push anything in anyones mouth.
- Don't assume you know better than them, they are actually the expert on what you are questioning them about.
- No wrong answers, let the people speak.

#### Be sensitive

- Be aware of who you have in front of you and who you are yourself.
- Questions about poverty while wearing designer clothes and walking around with the latest Iphone.
- Sensitive topics: cultural beliefs, age, politics, racism,...

#### Be respectfull

- Don't ask anything you wouldn't tell a stranger yourself.
  - If so, let them project it on a fictional case.

### Asking for permission

#### Informed concent

> A document about distribution, privacy and trust in the research.

Ethical duty to ensure that your user research does **not harm** the people who participate in them.

> Informed concent does **not** equal an NDA (non-disclosure achievement).

### Dark patterns of user research

- Credulity
- Dogmatism
- Biases
- Obscurantism
- Laziness
- Vagueness
- Hubris

#### Credulity

> Asking users what they want and believing the answer without proper proof.

People don't have reliable insight into their mental processes, so there is no point in asking them what they want.

**So instead of listening to what people say, look at what they do.**

#### Biases

> Bias means a special influence that sways one's thinking especially in a way considered to be unfair.

There is more than **180 cognitive biases**.  
They can lead to **dark patterns**.

#### Vagueness

> If you do research only occasionally, you need to answer lots of questions. This means you end up learning a little about a lot.

With user research you actually want to learn a lot about a little.  
That '_little_' questions is the specific question that keeps you up at night.

#### Hubris

> Hubris means extreme pride or self-confidence.

You show off the results but no one is interested in them.

## Working with children, minors, elderly, disadvantaged groups, ...

### COPRA (Children't Online Privacy Protection Act)

A federal in the United States that came into effect in 2000 and **protects personal information** of **children under 13**.

### For who?

**Websites or online services** that are directed to children under 13 years of age or who are collecting **personal information** online from a **child under 13 years** of age.

**Prohibiting them from doing unfair or deceptive acts or practices** in connection with the collection, use, and/or disclosure of personal information.

### Company responsibilities

They need to have a **privacy policy** listing the following:

- All **third parties** collecting children's personal information, e.g. social platform plug-ins, ad networks, etc.
- Description of **what personal information** is collected, and **how it's used**.
- Description of **parental rights**.

### Penalties

> **Google was fined $170 million** in 2019 for violations on **YouTube**, where children's personal information was collected without consent and used to target them with advertising.

> **$5.7 million** settlement agains Chinese company **ByteDance** owners of the **TikTok** app.

> Collective lawsuit against the **UK in 2020 for $3.2 billion** for similar violations of children's data privacy on **YouTube**.

### [Micropedia](https://www.themicropedia.org/#/) for micro-aggressions

#### What are micro-aggressions

Micro-aggressions are every day **snubs and insults** that marginalised groups face. (Micropedia)

> **Subtle comments** or actions that come from implicit **bias and/or stereotypes**.

- They might seem like compliments "You're beautiful for a transgender girl".
- A harmless comment "Where are you really from?".
- A subconscious action: crossing the street to avoid a racialised person.

#### How to avoid it

- Take the time to **think first**. _Micro-aggressions are so subtle, you often don't realise you're about to cause one but that doesn't mean you can't prevent them_.
- **Pause** for a moment.
- **Question** what you want to say/do and why.
- **Reframe your thoughts** on the bias you discovered through your questioning.

### Intigrate inclusion from the biginning

- When defining your user group: don't exclude anyone because of limitations, difficulties,...
- When defining your participants for primary research.
- When defining questions.
- When defining suggestions or advice.
